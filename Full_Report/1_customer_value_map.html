Customer value map

Identifying the most valuable households

We will answer the question: "Who are our most valuable households today and how are they distributed by size, share, and characteristics?"

Business framing

Were analyzing value at the household level so marketing, pricing, and retention teams can target wallets, coupons, and churn actions where they matter most. Transactions span Days 1711 (2017-01-01 to 2018-12-12), giving us a current view of spend and cadence. Well use business-facing RFM metrics and Lecture 3style clustering to map households into actionable segments for offers and outreach.

Quintile Scoring Applied:

  • Each metric scored 1-5 (quintiles)
  • Recency reversed (lower days = higher score)
  • Combined RFM score: 3-15

  • Champions (21.5% of HH) generate 47% of revenue; have a median recency of ~1 day and median household spend ~$5.9K (mean ~$7.2K) make them the clear revenue anchor.

Final answer to the business question

Our most valuable households right now are the 525 Champions highlighted by the RFM table and especially the 392 homes that the Lecture 3 cluster assigns to Cluster 2. They buy every few days (recency two days), place more than 250 baskets, spend a median of 9.7k dollars across the two years, and are mostly homeowners with at least one child. Champions alone deliver 47 percent of revenue and when we add Growth regulars the share rises to 64 percent, so this is the audience for concierge style coupons, exclusive bundles, and proactive service that Lecture 2 listed as strategic segmentation uses.

Clusters 0 and 3 show where the rest of the base sits because Cluster 0 still shops weekly and spends 3.4k dollars on median, meaning a nurture program can likely graduate them into Cluster 2, while Cluster 3 should receive reactivation pushes before they drift into Cluster 1 and vanish entirely.

Next steps focus on launching retention testing for Cluster 2, moving Cluster 0 households into a milestone loyalty journey, and running limited time win back offers for Cluster 3 that stress price matched essentials because 86 percent of Dormant households live there, which keeps us aligned with the test and learn mindset from Lecture 2 and the profiling guidance from Lecture 3.
